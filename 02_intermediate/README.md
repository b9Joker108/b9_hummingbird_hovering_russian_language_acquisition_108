## Overview

The intermediate level marks your transition from basic communication to genuine fluency. You'll engage with complex texts, express nuanced ideas, and deepen your cultural understanding.

### B1 Level (Threshold)
- Handle most situations while traveling
- Understand main points of clear standard input
- Produce simple connected text
- Describe experiences, events, dreams, hopes
- Express opinions with basic reasoning

### B2 Level (Vantage)
- Understand complex texts on concrete and abstract topics
- Interact with native speakers with fluency and spontaneity
- Produce detailed text on wide range of subjects
- Explain viewpoint with advantages and disadvantages
- Read liturgical texts with comprehension

## Grammar Focus Areas

### The Case System - Complete Mastery

All six cases in all contexts:

**Nominative** (Именительный) - Subject
**Genitive** (Родительный) - Possession, negation, quantities
**Dative** (Дательный) - Indirect object
**Accusative** (Винительный) - Direct object
**Instrumental** (Творительный) - Means, accompaniment
**Prepositional** (Предложный) - Location, about

**Practice Materials**: `/11_practice/intermediate/cases/`

### Verbal Aspect - Perfective/Imperfective

**Core Distinction**:
- **Imperfective** (несовершенный вид): Process, repetition, general action
- **Perfective** (совершенный вид): Completed action, result, one-time event

**Aspect Pairs**:
| Imperfective | Perfective | Meaning |
|--------------|-----------|---------|
| читать | прочитать | to read |
| писать | написать | to write |
| делать | сделать | to do/make |
| говорить | сказать | to speak/say |
| брать | взять | to take |

**Aspectual Nuances**:
- Future tense formation differences
- Use in negative contexts
- Imperative mood choices
- With modal verbs

### Verbs of Motion (Глаголы движения)

**Unidirectional vs. Multidirectional**:

| Multidirectional | Unidirectional | Meaning |
|------------------|----------------|---------|
| ходить | идти | to go (on foot) |
| ездить | ехать | to go (by vehicle) |
| бегать | бежать | to run |
| летать | лететь | to fly |
| носить | нести | to carry |
| водить | вести | to lead |
| плавать | плыть | to swim/sail |

**With Prefixes**: Create perfective and change meaning
- при- (arrival): прийти, приехать
- у- (departure): уйти, уехать
- вы- (exit): выйти, выехать
- в-/во- (entry): войти, въехать
- пере- (across): перейти, переехать

### Participles (Причастия)

**Four Types**:
1. **Present Active** (настоящее время, действительный залог)
   - читающий студент (the reading student)
2. **Past Active** (прошедшее время, действительный залог)
   - читавший студент (the student who was reading)
3. **Present Passive** (настоящее время, страдательный залог)
   - читаемая книга (the book being read)
4. **Past Passive** (прошедшее время, страдательный залог)
   - прочитанная книга (the read book)

**Usage**: Literary and formal style, creates complex attributive phrases

### Gerunds (Деепричастия)

**Two Types**:
1. **Imperfective** (несовершенный вид): читая (while reading)
2. **Perfective** (совершенный вид): прочитав (having read)

**Usage**: Express simultaneous or sequential actions
- *Читая книгу, он пил чай.* "While reading the book, he drank tea."
- *Прочитав книгу, он лёг спать.* "Having read the book, he went to bed."

### Word Formation

**Prefixes** changing meaning:
- **пре-**: превосходный (excellent)
- **раз-/рас-**: разделить (to divide)
- **без-/бес-**: бесконечный (infinite)

**Suffixes** creating derivatives:
- **-ость**: радость (joy), мудрость (wisdom)
- **-ние/-ение**: чтение (reading), учение (teaching)
- **-тель**: учитель (teacher), спаситель (savior)

### Idioms and Set Phrases

**Common Expressions**:
- *Бог даст* - God willing
- *С Богом!* - Go with God!
- *Спаси Господи!* - May the Lord save (you)! / Thank you!
- *Слава Богу!* - Thank God!

**Proverbs** (Пословицы):
- *Без труда не вытащишь и рыбку из пруда* - No pain, no gain
- *На Бога надейся, а сам не плошай* - Trust in God but do your part

### Grammatical Difficulties

**Aspect**: Choosing perfective vs. imperfective
- Solution: Study in context, note patterns

**Verbs of Motion**: Complex system
- Solution: Practice with prefixes systematically

**Cases**: Advanced usage (especially Instrumental)
- Solution: Read extensively, note patterns

### Vocabulary Expansion

**Passive to Active**: Recognizing vs. producing
- Solution: Active recall practice, speaking

**Register Appropriateness**: Formal vs. informal
- Solution: Read varied sources, note contexts

# The Cyrillic Alphabet: Sacred Origins to Modern Use

*Азбука - От святых Кирилла и Мефодия*

## Historical Development

### Pre-Cyrillic Era
Before 863 CE, Slavic peoples used:
- Various runic systems (see `/13_runes/`)
- "Черты и резы" (lines and cuts)
- Adapted Greek letters (informal)
- Oral tradition predominantly

### The Holy Brothers: Cyril and Methodius

**Saints Cyril (Constantine) and Methodius** (Кирилл и Мефодий)
- **Mission**: 863 CE to Great Moravia
- **Purpose**: Liturgical translation for Slavic peoples
- **Achievement**: Created Glagolitic alphabet (first Slavic alphabet)
- **Legacy**: Cyrillic alphabet named after St. Cyril

**Feast Day**: May 24 (celebrated as Slavic Script and Culture Day)

### From Glagolitic to Cyrillic

**Timeline**:
1. **863 CE** - Glagolitic created (round, complex forms)
2. **Late 9th C** - Cyrillic develops from Glagolitic + Greek uncial
3. **988 CE** - Baptism of Rus' - Cyrillic spreads
4. **10-11th C** - Church Slavonic standardizes
5. **1708-1710** - Peter the Great's civil alphabet reform
6. **1918** - Bolshevik orthography reform
7. **1991-present** - Post-Soviet period, minor adjustments

### Special Characters

**Ъ (твёрдый знак)** - Hard Sign
- Separates consonant from following soft vowel
- No sound of its own
- Example: объект (ob-yekt), подъезд (entrance)

**Ь (мягкий знак)** - Soft Sign  
- Softens (palatalizes) preceding consonant
- No sound of its own
- Example: мать (mother), учитель (teacher)

**Ё (ё)** - Always Stressed
- Always stressed in a word
- Often written as Е in casual writing (but pronounced ё)
- Example: всё (everything), ёлка (fir tree)

## Church Slavonic Letters (Historical)

Some letters exist in Church Slavonic but not modern Russian:

| Letter | Name | Sound | Notes |
|--------|------|-------|-------|
| Ѣ ѣ | ять | "ye" or "e" | Abolished 1918 |
| Ѳ ѳ | фита | "f" | Abolished 1918, from Greek theta |
| Ѵ ѵ | ижица | "i" or "v" | Abolished 1918, rare even before |
| І і | и десятеричное | "i" | Merged with И |
| Ѕ ѕ | зело | "dz" | Archaic |
| Ѿ | от | "ot" | Ligature |
| Ѡ ѡ | омега | "o" | From Greek omega |
| Ѧ ѧ | малый юс | nasal "ya" | Very old |
| Ѫ ѫ | большой юс | nasal "u" | Very old |

**Note**: These appear in liturgical texts and historical documents.

## Letter Pronunciation Rules

### Vowel Reduction

**Stressed vs. Unstressed**:
- **О** → "ah" when unstressed: *молоко́* [məlɐˈko] "milk"
- **Е** → "i" when unstressed: *весна́* [vʲɪˈsna] "spring"
- **А** → "uh" when far from stress: *сáмовар* [ˈsaməvər]

**Regional Difference**:
- **Moscow/Southern** (аканье): О → А in unstressed position
- **Northern** (оканье): О preserved in all positions

### Consonant Rules

**Voicing/Devoicing**:
- Final consonants devoice: *Бог* [Бох] "God"
- Consonant clusters assimilate: *водка* [вотка] "vodka"

**Soft vs. Hard Consonants**:
- Soft before: Е, Ё, И, Ю, Я, Ь
- Hard before: А, О, У, Ы, Э, Ъ
- Always hard: Ж, Ш, Ц
- Always soft: Ч, Щ, Й

## Writing Systems

### Print (Printed Letters)

Standard printed forms used in books and typed text.

### Cursive (Handwriting - Прописные буквы)

**Important**: Russian cursive differs significantly from print!

Key differences:
- **т** (print) → looks like **m** (cursive)
- **и** (print) → looks like **u** (cursive)  
- **д** (print) → looks like **g** (cursive)
- Letters connect in flowing strokes

**Practice Essential**: Must learn cursive for handwriting.

### Typography Styles

- **Устав** (Uncial) - Old Church Slavonic, formal
- **Полуустав** (Semi-uncial) - Medieval manuscripts
- **Скоропись** (Cursive) - Faster writing, 14th C onward
- **Гражданский шрифт** (Civil script) - Peter the Great's reform (1708)
- **Вязь** (Vyaz) - Decorative ligature script (see `/14_iconography/`)

## Sacred Aspects

### Alphabetic Theology

In Eastern Christian tradition, the alphabet itself holds sacred meaning:

**А (Alpha)** - Beginning: *"Я есмь Альфа"* "I am the Alpha" (Rev 1:8)
**Я (closest to Omega)** - Related to first-person "I"

**The Word (Слово)**:
> *"В начале было Слово"* - "In the beginning was the Word" (John 1:1)

The alphabet enables writing sacred Scripture in Slavonic.

### Saints Cyril and Methodius Prayer

**In Russian**:
> *Святые равноапостольные Кирилл и Мефодий, молите Бога о нас!*
> "Holy equals-to-the-apostles Cyril and Methodius, pray to God for us!"

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Confusing letters**: В/Б, Н/И, Ш/Щ
2. **English assumptions**: Р ≠ P, Н ≠ H, С ≠ C
3. **Ignoring soft/hard**: Changes meaning: *мат* vs *мать*
4. **Skipping cursive**: Essential for reading handwriting
5. **Ignoring stress**: Changes pronunciation significantly

# Russian Phonetics Fundamentals

*Фонетика русского языка*

## Overview

Russian phonetics is systematic but differs significantly from English. Mastering pronunciation early prevents fossilization of errors and enables authentic communication.

## Vowel System (Гласные)

### Russian Vowels: 10 Letters, 5-6 Phonemes

**Written vowels**: А, О, У, Ы, Э, Я, Ё, Ю, И, Е (10 letters)
**Phonemic vowels**: /a/, /o/, /u/, /i/, /e/, /ɨ/ (5-6 sounds)

### Vowel Pairs (Hard-Soft)

| Hard Vowel | Soft Vowel | Effect |
|------------|-----------|---------|
| А | Я | Softens preceding consonant |
| О | Ё | Softens preceding consonant |
| У | Ю | Softens preceding consonant |
| Э | Е | Softens preceding consonant |
| Ы | И | Softens preceding consonant |

### Vowel Reduction (Редукция)

**Key Principle**: Only stressed vowels are pronounced fully.

#### O-reduction (Аканье - Moscow pattern)
- **Stressed О**: [o] - *дом* [dom] "house"
- **Pre-stress О**: [ɐ] - *мова́* [mɐˈva] "Moscow"
- **Other unstressed О**: [ə] - *молоко́* [məlɐˈko] "milk"

#### E-reduction
- **Stressed Е**: [e] - *лес* [lʲes] "forest"
- **Unstressed Е**: [ɪ] - *весна́* [vʲɪˈsna] "spring"

#### A-reduction
- **Stressed А**: [a] - *мать* [matʲ] "mother"
- **Pre-stress А**: [ɐ] - *вода́* [vɐˈda] "water"
- **Other unstressed А**: [ə] - *самова́р* [səmɐˈvar] "samovar"

### Regional Variations

**Аканье** (Southern/Moscow):
- Unstressed О → [ɐ] or [ə]
- Standard literary pronunciation
- Example: *хорошо́* [xərɐˈʂo]

**Оканье** (Northern):
- О retained in all positions
- Old regional dialect feature
- Example: *хорошо́* [xoroˈʂo]

**Note**: Standard Russian uses аканье.

## Consonant System (Согласные)

### Consonant Inventory

**21 consonant letters**: Б, В, Г, Д, Ж, З, Й, К, Л, М, Н, П, Р, С, Т, Ф, Х, Ц, Ч, Ш, Щ

**Phonemes**: ~37 (due to hard/soft pairs)

### Hard vs. Soft Consonants (Palatalization)

**Most Important Feature**: Almost all consonants come in hard/soft pairs.

#### How Softening Works

**Soft (palatalized)**: Tongue raised toward hard palate (like adding "y" sound)
- **Example**: *мать* [matʲ] "mother" vs *мат* [mat] "swear word"

**Indicators of Softness**:
1. Soft vowels: Я, Ё, Ю, И, Е
2. Soft sign: Ь
3. Another soft consonant (sometimes)

#### Paired Consonants

| Hard | Soft | Example (hard) | Example (soft) |
|------|------|---------------|----------------|
| [b] | [bʲ] | *был* (was) | *бить* (to beat) |
| [v] | [vʲ] | *вол* (ox) | *вёл* (led) |
| [g] | [gʲ] | *гол* (goal) | *гель* (gel) |
| [d] | [dʲ] | *дал* (gave) | *дядя* (uncle) |
| [z] | [zʲ] | *зал* (hall) | *зять* (son-in-law) |
| [k] | [kʲ] | *кров* (shelter) | *кювет* (ditch) |
| [l] | [lʲ] | *лук* (onion) | *люк* (hatch) |
| [m] | [mʲ] | *мать* (to crumple) | *мять* (to crumple) |
| [n] | [nʲ] | *кон* (stake) | *конь* (horse) |
| [p] | [pʲ] | *пыл* (ardor) | *пил* (drank) |
| [r] | [rʲ] | *рад* (glad) | *ряд* (row) |
| [s] | [sʲ] | *сэр* (sir) | *сер* (gray) |
| [t] | [tʲ] | *тук* (knock) | *тюк* (bale) |
| [f] | [fʲ] | *фон* (background) | *Фёдор* (Theodore) |
| [x] | [xʲ] | *хор* (choir) | *хитрый* (cunning) |

#### Always Hard (Never Soft)

- **Ж** [ʐ] - *жизнь* "life"
- **Ш** [ʂ] - *школа* "school"
- **Ц** [ts] - *царь* "tsar"

**Exception**: Despite being written with И (ЖИ, ШИ, ЦИ), these remain hard!

#### Always Soft (Never Hard)

- **Ч** [tɕ] - *час* "hour"
- **Щ** [ɕː] - *щит* "shield"
- **Й** [j] - *май* "May"

### Voice and Devoicing

#### Voiced-Voiceless Pairs

| Voiced | Voiceless | Example |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| Б [b] | П [p] | *бар*/*пар* (bar/steam) |
| В [v] | Ф [f] | *вата*/*фата* (cotton/veil) |
| Г [g] | К [k] | *год*/*кот* (year/cat) |
| Д [d] | Т [t] | *дом*/*том* (house/volume) |
| Ж [ʐ] | Ш [ʂ] | *жар*/*шар* (heat/ball) |
| З [z] | С [s] | *зуб*/*суп* (tooth/soup) |

#### Devoicing Rules

**Final Devoicing**: Voiced consonants at word end → voiceless
- *хлеб* [xlʲep] "bread" (written Б, pronounced П)
- *Бог* [box] "God" (written Г, pronounced К)
- *муж* [muʂ] "husband" (written Ж, pronounced Ш)

**Assimilation in Clusters**: Consonants assimilate to following consonant
- *водка* [ˈvotkə] "vodka" (Д → Т before К)
- *сдать* [sdatʲ] "to hand in" (С → З before Д)

### Specific Sounds

#### Р [r] - Trilled R
**Most challenging sound for English speakers**
- Alveolar trill (tongue tip vibrates)
- Different from English "r"
- Essential to practice

**Practice**:
- Start with "д-д-д-р-р-р"
- Try "трррактор" (tractor)
- Voiced: *рука́* (hand)
- Soft: *рядом* (nearby) [rʲ]

#### Х [x] - Voiceless Velar Fricative
- Like Scottish "loch" or German "Bach"
- **Not** like English "h"
- Back of tongue to soft palate

**Examples**:
- *храм* [xram] "temple"
- *хорошо́* [xərɐˈʂo] "good, well"
- *хлеб* [xlʲep] "bread"

#### Ы [ɨ] - Hard I
**Most difficult Russian vowel for foreigners**
- Retracted high vowel
- No English equivalent
- Back of tongue raised, lips neutral

**Practice**:
- Say "i" (as in "bit"), pull tongue back
- Compare: *мы* [mɨ] "we" vs *ми* [mʲi] "mi" (music note)
- *сын* [sɨn] "son"

#### Ж [ʐ] / Ш [ʂ] - Retroflex Fricatives
- Tongue tip curled back
- Harder/darker than English "zh" and "sh"
- Always hard (even before И)

**Examples**:
- *жизнь* [ʐɨznʲ] "life"
- *широкий* [ʂɨˈrokʲɪj] "wide"

#### Щ [ɕː] - Long Soft "Shch"
**Two pronunciations**:
1. Moscow: Long soft [ɕː] (like elongated soft "sh")
2. St. Petersburg/Old: [ʂtɕ] (sh-ch sequence)

**Examples**:
- *щи* [ɕːi] "cabbage soup"
- *ещё* [jɪˈɕːo] "still, yet"

#### Ц [ts] - Affricate
- Like "ts" in "cats"
- Always hard
- Single sound, not two

**Examples**:
- *царь* [tsarʲ] "tsar"
- *цирк* [tsɨrk] "circus" (note: Ы sound after Ц)

#### Ч [tɕ] - Soft Affricate
- Like "ch" in "church" but softer
- Always soft
- Single sound

**Examples**:
- *час* [tɕas] "hour"
- *человек* [tɕɪlɐˈvʲek] "person"

## Stress (Ударение)

### Key Principles

1. **Free Stress**: Can fall on any syllable
2. **Mobile Stress**: Changes in declension/conjugation
3. **Unpredictable**: Must be learned per word
4. **Crucial**: Changes meaning

### Stress Marking

**In dictionaries/learning**: Acute accent over vowel
- *замо́к* [zɐˈmok] "lock"
- *за́мок* [ˈzamək] "castle"

**In regular text**: Not marked (except Ё, always stressed)

### Stress and Meaning

| Word | Stress | Meaning |
|------|--------|---------|
| мукá | final | flour |
| му́ка | initial | torment |
| óрган | initial | organ (body part) |
| оргáн | final | organ (instrument) |

### Stress Patterns in Grammar

**Nouns**: Can shift
- *рука́* (hand) → *ру́ки* (hands)
- *стена́* (wall) → *сте́ны* (walls)

**Verbs**: Often shifts
- *писа́ть* (to write) → *я пишу́* (I write)

## Intonation (Интонация)

### Intonation Constructions (IK)

Russian linguists identify 7 main intonation patterns:

**IK-1**: Completeness (statement)
- Neutral statement, downward at end
- *Это книга.* "This is a book."

**IK-2**: Specification (WH-question)
- WH-questions, commands
- Sharp rise-fall on stressed syllable
- *Что это?* "What is this?"

**IK-3**: Incompleteness (yes/no question)
- High rise on stressed syllable
- *Это книга?* "Is this a book?"

**IK-4**: Official/formal question
- *А у вас?* "And at your place?"

**IK-5**: Evaluation/impression
- *Какой дом!* "What a house!"

**IK-6**: Exaggeration
- *Какой умный!* (ironic) "How smart!" (sarcastic)

**IK-7**: Negation-refutation
- *Нет!* (emphatic) "No!"

## Phonetic Processes

### Assimilation

**Voicing assimilation**:
- *отдать* → [ɐdˈdatʲ] (Т → Д before Д)
- *сделать* → [ˈzdʲelətʲ] (С → З before Д)

**Softness assimilation**:
- Some consonants soften before soft consonants
- *степь* [stʲepʲ] - both Т and П soft

### Elision

**Fast speech reduction**:
- *здравствуйте* [ˈzdrastvujtʲɪ] → often [ˈzdrastʲɪ]
- *сейчас* [sʲɪˈtɕas] → often [ˈsʲɛɕːas] or [ˈsʲas]

## Common Mistakes

### For English Speakers

1. **Not distinguishing hard/soft**: Critical in Russian!
2. **English "r" instead of trilled [r]**
3. **Pronouncing unstressed O as [o]**: Use аканье
4. **Ignoring voicing rules**: Final devoicing essential
5. **Wrong stress**: Changes meaning dramatically
6. **Aspirated p, t, k**: Russian consonants are unaspirated

## Practice Strategies

### Minimal Pairs

Practice distinguishing:
- *мать* [matʲ] / *мат* [mat] - mother / swear word
- *был* [bɨl] / *бил* [bʲil] - was / beat
- *лук* [luk] / *люк* [lʲuk] - onion / hatch

### Tongue Twisters (Скороговорки)

**For Р**:
> *На дворе трава, на траве дрова*
> "In the yard is grass, on the grass is wood"

**For Ш/Ж**:
> *Шла Саша по шоссе и сосала сушку*
> "Sasha walked along the highway sucking on a bagel"

**For hard/soft**:
> *Карл у Клары украл кораллы*
> "Karl stole corals from Clara"

### Recording Practice

1. Record native speaker
2. Record yourself
3. Compare carefully
4. Repeat until similar

### Liturgical Pronunciation

**Church Slavonic** has some different features:
- More careful vowel pronunciation
- Different stress patterns sometimes
- Formal, elevated style

## Phonetic Transcription

### Standard Systems

**IPA** (International Phonetic Alphabet): Used internationally
**Russian phonetic transcription**: Used in Russian linguistics

**Example** - *здравствуйте* "hello":
- Careful: [ˈzdrastvujtʲɪ]
- Casual: [ˈzdrastʲɪ]

## Cultural Context

### Regional Accents

**Moscow**: Standard, аканье
**St. Petersburg**: More "refined", щ as [ʂtɕ]
**Southern**: Stronger аканье, voiced final consonants
**Northern**: Оканье, different palatalization

### Church vs. Secular

**Liturgical**: More careful, full vowels
**Conversational**: More reduction, faster
